Who Is Tara Schiphof? Miss Georgia 2023: Age &amp; Wiki

Publish date: 2024-05-01

Tara Schiphof, a 25-year-old from King, North Carolina, was chosen as Miss Georgia 2023 on Saturday, June 17, 2023, defeating 57 other candidates. At Columbus, Georgia’s River Center of Performing Arts, the pageant’s Grand Finale took place.

Tara Schiphof has crowned Miss Georgia in 2023 and also got a sash, a title, and a $25,000 scholarship. Kelsey Hollis, Miss Georgia 2022 and the current queen was named Miss Capital City. Tara will represent Georgia in the Miss America 2024 competition as the 21st state champion. Her participation in the Miss Georgia Scholarship Competition was notable because it was her second time. Last year, she was selected as the first runner-up.

Ballet is performed by Tara Schiphof

Melanie Oakley and Chris Schiphof, Tara’s parents, who both still live in King, North Carolina, were there when she was born. Before transferring to Chestnut Grove Middle School, Tara attended Mount Olive Elementary School. She received the prestigious Stokes County Arts Council Scholarship, allowing her to complete both high school and college.

2019 saw Tara graduate from the University of North Carolina School of Arts with a Bachelor of Fine Arts in classical dance. In 2020, she also intends to graduate from Wake Forest University’s School of Business Master of Science in Management program. She belonged to the school’s dance squad.

Since she was a young child, Tara Schiphof has danced. She has performed with Dance Perfections, shown off her outstanding ballet skills at Stokes Stomp, King Fest, and Walnut Cove Festival in her hometown, and done it all. She moved to Georgia to work as a marketing specialist for Stanley Black & Decker. At age 23, Tara Schiphof became the first woman in the state to win the Miss Macon competition.

Miss Georgia’s 2023 community service initiative is called #YesYouCan

Miss Georgia 2023 stood out from the competition thanks to her #YesYouCan civic engagement initiative. It is about giving failure new meaning and overcoming the negativity that accompanies the idea of failure.

In her declaration of triumph, Tara stated:

I’ve been able to give students scholarships through #YesYouCan and assist others in acquiring the abilities necessary to persevere in the face of some of life’s most challenging obstacles. I genuinely want to change the way we view failure and turn it into a chance for growth rather than something to be terrified of.

She continued by saying that one of the best things about her curriculum is that everyone can relate to it and benefit from it, regardless of their age, background, or demographic. She continued by saying that she intended to distribute it to all Georgian schools. In addition, Tara credited her own tenacity for helping her succeed, saying:

“I learned the value of perseverance when I was 11 years old because I participated in a competition and it took me nine years to win.” It taught me how to set small goals and accomplish with them. It showed me why the journey itself is far more important than the end result.
