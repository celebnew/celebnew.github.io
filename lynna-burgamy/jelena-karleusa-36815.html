Biography

Jelena Karleusa was born in Belgrade, Yugoslavia on August 17, 1978. Serbian pop singer and columnist who became popular after releasing her debut turbo-folk album, Ogledalce, in 1995. She became a judge on the singing competition, Zvezda Granda, in 2015. She used her newspaper column in Kurir to critique such politicians as Boris Tadic and Ivica Dačić. Her debut album sold upwards of 100,000 copies across Serbia and Montenegro, establishing her as a true pop star. She went on to have several charts hits, collaborating with Marina Tucaković much of the time.

Career

In the mid-nineties, Karleuša became one of the leading regional performers in the following decades with a number of successful albums, including Samo za tvoje oči (2002), Magija (2005), JK Revolution (2008), and Diva (2012), and two major Belgrade concerts: All About Diva (2010) and Viva la Diva Show (2013). Subsequently, after facing setbacks, she managed to revive her career by gaining influence over her fashion style in social media, becoming the first Serbian personality after Novak Djokovic to reach one million followers on Instagram. Karleuša also became a judge of the popular televised singing competition, Zvezda Granda, in 2015.
